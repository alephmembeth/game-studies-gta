\section{The GTA Corpus}\label{sec:corpus}
Text files for \textit{Grand Theft Auto} (1997), \textit{Grand Theft Auto 2} (1999), \textit{Grand Theft Auto III} (2001), \textit{Grand Theft Auto: Vice City} (2002), \textit{Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas} (2004), \textit{Grand Theft Auto Advance} (2004), \textit{Grand Theft Auto: Liberty City Stories} (2005), \textit{Grand Theft Auto: Vice City Stories} (2006), and \textit{Grand Theft Auto: Chinatown Wars} (2009) were sourced from the internet.
Files for \textit{Grand Theft Auto IV} (2008), \textit{Grand Theft Auto IV: The Lost and Damned} (2009), \textit{Grand Theft Auto IV: The Ballad of Gay Tony} (2009), as well as \textit{Grand Theft Auto V} (2013) have been identified, decompressed, decrypted, and converted from the proprietary GXT2 archives using the OpenIV utility.
The extraction targeted specific language repositories within the game's file system to ensure a comprehensive capture of English strings (for a full list of files as well as their sources in the web or the game files, see \autoref{sec:app_corpus}).

The $950$ files share a consistent underlying structure:
They begin with metadata headers (e.\,g., containing version information), followed by a line-by-line mapping of unique keys to specific text content.
Furthermore, the text contains internal markup, such as color codes and formatting tags.
In its raw state, the corpus appears as shown below.

\begin{table}[h!]
\begin{tabular}{lr}
   Metric              & Value               \\\hline\hline
   Lines               &    $631,033$        \\
   Characters          & $27,877,767$        \\
   Tokens              &  $4,131,031$        \\
   Unique Word Types   &    $769,730$        \\
   Type-Token Ratio    &          $0.1863$   \\\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\noindent We used regular expressions and heuristic filters to systematically eliminate technical noise from the dataset.
Specifically, tilde-wrapped sequences, underscored identifiers, strings consisting entirely of uppercase letters, as well as purely numeric sequences were stripped.\footnote{The Python script is available from \url{https://github.com/alephmembeth/conspiracy-gta/tree/main/analysis}.}
After cleaning the data in this way, the processed corpus looks as follows.

\begin{table}[h!]
\begin{tabular}{lrr}
   Metric              & Value               & Difference           \\\hline\hline
   Lines               &    $367,301$        &    $-263,732$        \\
   Characters          & $15,347,302$        & $-12,530,465$        \\
   Tokens              &  $2,686,957$        &  $-1,444,074$        \\
   Unique Word Types   &     $45,697$        &    $-724,033$        \\
   Type-Token Ratio    &          $0.0170$   &          $-0.1693$   \\\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}
